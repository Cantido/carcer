Inscribed along a cave wall in [[Wavetide Hollow]], in Deep Speech script, revealed under moonlit water, a riddle reads:

> 5 fathoms of the one so deep
> Eb from tides that hold and keep
> A current to find the ancient tomb
> and cousins unite in the salt-born womb
> 
> No mourning of loss for the cousin so grim
> The shallows cursed, no longer safe to swim
> But if you can prove he is not alone
> Then once more the shores our children be shown
> 
> To find these Fathoms one must climb and dive
> To not only locate, but activate the 5
> Some may be carried, their weight on your back
> Others a challenge, a riddle, an attack
>
> But if you succeed the way will be clear
> and down to the depths, you go with fear
> For while he is freed, and no longer bound
> Solitude is unkind, will he want to be found?

A related monument reads:

> Here we honor who stilled the waves,
> Who taught the deep to spare, and not to crave.
> He bore no blade, he built no throne,
> Yet made the tides a place called home.
> 
> To fins and shells and voices small,
> He listened long and lifted all.
> His hands were kind, his reach was wide,
> He swam with stars and turned the tide.
> 
> Cousin bound by blood and cursed by chain,
> family sank beneath the surf in pain.
> Not dead, not lost, but sealed away—
> A prisoner still in ocean’s sway.
> 
> Speak name with breath held tight,
> When waves go wrong and wrong feels right.
> For the sea is not cruel, but it always knows…
> Where silence sleeps and sorrow grows.
